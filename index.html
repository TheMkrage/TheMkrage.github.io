Meet the Bots

Meet the Bots

We're fascinated with robots because they are reflections of ourselves.

Vulcan

Vulcan was our 2017 robot that competed in FIRST Steamworks. With its completely automatic turreted shooter, Vulcan could track and shoot up to 8 balls a second into the boiler.
Awards:
- Chairman's Award (Arizona)
- Tournament Finalists (Los Angeles)
- Tournament Champions (Orange)
- Innovation in Control (Los Angeles)
- #2 Seed in our Division (World Champs)

Obsidian

Obsidian was our 2016 robot that competed in FIRST Stronghold. Hosted on a Raspberry Pi, Obsidian's vision tracking system helped it to aim and fire a ball into the goal.
Awards:
- Chairman's Award (Los Angeles)
- Tournament Champions (Orange)
- Innovation in Control (Orange)
- Divisional Quarterfinalists (World Champs)
- Tournament Champions (Beach Blitz)

Robie II

Robie II was our second VEX Robot built for the Nothing but Net season. I was heavily involved in the design process in addition to programming Robie II. Robie II uses feed forward speed controllers and a sin smoothing drive equation.
Awards:
- 2x Excellence Award
- 4x Tournament Champions
- 1x Programming Skills Winner
- 1x Innovate Award
- 1x Tournament Finalists
- State Finalists

Totebot

Totebot was built to compete in an offseason competition, which our team helped organize with other teams in the area. Each team built their own totebot and met to race.
Awards:
- Tournament Champions

ArisTOTEle

ArisTOTEle was a robot built for the 2015 FRC season, Recycle Rush. Some main features of ArisTOTEle include a system to move two separate carriages in sync and an autonomous that scores the max amount of points.
Awards:
- Innovation in Control (Las Vegas)
- Tournament Finalists (Las Vegas)
- Tournament Champions (Arizona)
- Quality Awards (World Champs)
- Programming Award (Chezy Champs)

Robie

Robie was a robot built for the 2014-2015 VEX game Skyrise (our rookie vex year). Robie was the first robot that I programmed. Through programming Robie, I learned the basics of PID and common design patterns of C.
Awards:
- 2x Excellence Award
- 2x Tournament Champions
- 2x Programming Awards
- Robot Skills State Champions
- State Championship Amaze Award (Given Because of our Autonomous)
- Quarterfinalists (State Champs)
