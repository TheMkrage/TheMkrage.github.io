Friar Scout

Friar Scout was an iOS app that gave the scouters of Team 3309 the ability to scout from their phones. It also organized and presented the scouting information. With Friar Scout, scouting at FIRST Regionals has never been easier!

VEX Scout

VEX Scout is an iOS app designed to bring all the tools for VEX scouting to your fingertips. VEX Scout capitalizes on pre- scouting. The app allows users to search through scores, averages, teams, competitions, awards, and much more. VEX Scout uses up-to-date statistics, making sure only accurate data is received. With VEX Scout anyone can sharpen their scouting skills and take their team to the next level.

Team 3309's Website

A new and updated website for Team 3309's robotics program. It contains loads of information about our program and ways you can get involved. It also hosts the training program where new members can take courses and track their progress. This allows the verterans to have the ability to see who is trained in certain areas, which allows for easy delegation of jobs.

We're fascinated with robots because they are reflections of ourselves.

Gateway

Gateway was a robot built for the 2014 FRC game Aerial Assist. Because I was just a freshman when Gateway was created, I only worked to assemble Gateway.

Awards: - Engineering Inspiration** (Los Angeles Regional)

Robie

Robie was a robot built for the 2014-2015 VEX game Skyrise (our rookie vex year). Robie was the first robot that I worked to program. Through programming Robie, I learned the basics of Proportional Integral Derivative Controllers. Robie taught me skills that have been applicable to almost every other project I have worked on.
Awards:
- 2x Excellence Award*
- 2x Tournament Champions*
- 2x Think Award
- 2x Programming Skills Winner
- 2x Robot Skills Winner
- 1x Innovate Award
- Robot Skills State Champions**
- State Championship Amaze Award
- Quarter-finalists at State Championships

ArisTOTEle

ArisTOTEle was a robot built for the 2015 FRC season Recycle Rush. ArisTOTEle was the first FRC Robot I programmed. Some main features of ArisTOTEle include 2 separate PID loops tuned to the same speed that allow two intakes on separate rails to move together, an autonomous that scores the max amount of points, and a drive equation that uses angular velocity and PID to turn quickly.
Awards:
- Excellence in Engineering (Los Angeles Regional)
- Quarter-finalists (Los Angeles Regional)
- Innovation in Control (Los Vegas Regional)
- Tournament Finalists** (Los Vegas Regional)
- Tournament Champions** (Arizona Regional)
- Excellence in Engineering (Arizona Regional)
- Quality Awards (World Champs)
- Divisional Quarter-finalists (World Champs)
- Programming Award (Chezy Champs)

Robie II

Robie II was our second VEX Robot built for the Nothing but Net season. I was heavily involved in the design process of Robie II in addition to programming it. Robie II has a feed forward speed controller on the flywheel as well as a drive equation which uses sin smoothing.
